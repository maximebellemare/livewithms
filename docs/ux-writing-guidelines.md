# UX Writing Guidelines

LiveWithMS copy should be calm through clarity, not through extra narration.

## Core rule

Before adding any sentence, ask:

- Is this useful?
- Is this concrete?
- Is this understandable instantly?
- Does this reduce confusion?
- Does this provide real value?

If the answer is no, do not include it.

## Every sentence should do at least one of these

- Explain functionality
- Clarify meaning
- Communicate a real feature
- Improve discoverability
- Provide practical guidance
- Communicate a concrete benefit

## Avoid

- Vague emotional narration
- Philosophical product commentary
- Poetic or reflective filler
- Ambient reassurance
- AI-sounding soft language
- Pseudo-profound statements
- “Calmness narration” that describes a mood instead of a feature

## Prefer

- Short sentences
- Concrete nouns and verbs
- Practical phrasing
- Direct descriptions of what the feature does
- Clear state labels
- Low reading effort for fatigue and brain fog

## Examples

Avoid:

- “A quieter pace may help here.”
- “There’s a steadier stretch of history here now.”
- “Financial accessibility should remain part of the design over time.”

Prefer:

- “Reduce decisions and narrow the next step.”
- “Check-in history is available across 12 saved entries.”
- “Billing support options may be available during difficult periods.”

## Product tone

The product should feel:

- Calm
- Intelligent
- Restrained
- Trustworthy
- Premium through simplicity

The product should not feel:

- Abstract
- Reflective for its own sake
- Emotionally decorative
- Self-narrating
- AI-generated
